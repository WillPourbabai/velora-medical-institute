# Velora Voice Reference

Extracted from the three strongest KEEP sections — voice rules for any rewrite.

## Source sections

1. **Home / "Our Approach"** (`app/page.tsx` 27–76)
2. **Home / "Why Velora"** numbered list (`app/page.tsx` 248–275)
3. **Hormone Therapy / "Important Information"** (`app/hormone-therapy/page.tsx` 222–251)

## Sentence rhythm

- Long → short → long. Hero claim, then a 4–8 word reinforcement, then a longer mechanism.
- Em-dashes carry the load. Used to slot in the *because* without a new sentence.
  - "Treatment plans are individualized — based on clinical evaluation and response."
  - "Care directed by physicians — not protocols."
- Lists are crisp. Each bullet is a noun-phrase or a clause, never a full mini-paragraph.
- 3 paragraphs is the natural unit for body copy: setup, mechanism, payoff.

## Formality + register

- Clinical-formal but plain. No marketing bravado, no sales adjectives ("amazing," "transformative," "revolutionary"). Also no PR hedging ("we strive to," "passionate about").
- Uses bare nouns where most clinics use puffery: "evaluation," "monitoring," "adjustment," "follow-up," "response."
- Comfortable with the word "physician." Avoids "experts," "team," "specialists" except where clinically true.
- Comfortable saying what the practice does *not* do ("not based on fixed protocols," "not appropriate for all patients"). This is a signature move.

## Directness vs. hedging

- Hedges only where medicine requires it ("may benefit," "when appropriate," "individual results vary").
- Otherwise direct: "Velora is built differently." "Hormone therapy requires careful oversight." "Medication costs are not included in visit fees."
- States operational facts up front; doesn't bury them.

## Sentence length

- Average ~16 words. Range 4–34.
- Headlines are tight: 4–10 words ("Care directed by physicians — not protocols.").
- Body sentences vary; long sentences are rare and earned.

## Signature moves

1. **Contrast pair**: "X is not Y. X is Z." ("Most clinics operate on standardized templates… Velora is built differently.")
2. **Em-dash for mechanism**: leading clause + em-dash + the *how* or *why*.
3. **Short clinical noun list** instead of adjective-stacked claims ("comprehensive evaluation, evidence-based prescribing, ongoing monitoring").
4. **Eyebrow + headline contains italicized accent** in `text-gold` or `text-sage` — the italic always carries the stress word ("metabolic", "physicians", "stated up front").
5. **Numbered or bulleted lists with mechanism in the body**, not the title. Title is the claim; body is the *because*.
6. **"Physician-guided / physician-directed"** is the brand verb. Always reach for it before "specialist," "expert," or "team."

## What to avoid (anti-patterns the strong sections never do)

- "Tailored to your biology and goals." (vague, swap-test fail)
- "Expert guidance every step." (claim with no mechanism)
- "Optimize your health. Elevate your life." (cosmetic-brand register, not medical)
- "Personalized care designed around your health, your goals, and your life." (true of any practice)
- "We're here to help." (filler; says nothing)
- "Get started." (CTA without next concrete step)
- Standalone trust badges that are platitudes ("Focused On You").

## Concrete examples to model rewrites on

**Headline pattern:** `"<verb-phrase>, <em>not</em> <opposite>."`
- "Sustained improvement, *not* short-term change."
- "Care directed by *physicians*, not protocols."

**Body pattern:** `<setup>. <mechanism>. <consequence>.`
- "Most weight-loss and hormone clinics operate on standardized templates: a fixed protocol, a fixed cadence, a fixed prescription. That model can produce short-term wins, but it rarely produces durable, individualized results."

**Bullet pattern:** `<noun-claim>` + `<one-sentence mechanism in body>`.
- Title: "Safe prescribing with appropriate monitoring"
- Body: "Medication selection is grounded in evidence and clinical safety."

**Operational fact pattern:** `<bold short fact> + <em-dash or full stop> + <consequence>.`
- "**Medication costs are not included** in visit fees."
- "**Hormone therapy is not appropriate for all patients.** Eligibility is determined through clinical evaluation."

## Rule for new copy

If a sentence is still true after replacing "Velora" / "telemedicine" with any other business or service, cut it. Replace it with a sentence that names a concrete audience, a concrete mechanism, or a concrete operational fact.
